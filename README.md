# Advanced High Strength Steels: Heat Treatment Design

A study on the role of heat treatment in designing Advanced High Strength Steels (AHSS) used in modern automotive structures.  
This project explores how microstructure engineering improves strength, ductility, and crash performance.

Developed as part of the Materials Engineering coursework at IIT Gandhinagar.

---

## Project Goal

To understand how different heat treatment processes control microstructure and mechanical properties in advanced steels such as Dual Phase (DP), TRIP, and Quenching & Partitioning (Q&P) steels.

---

## Key Steel Types Studied

• **Dual Phase (DP) Steel**  
Ferrite matrix with martensite islands providing high strength and good formability.

• **TRIP Steel**  
Contains retained austenite that transforms to martensite during deformation, improving ductility.

• **Quenching & Partitioning (Q&P) Steel**  
Uses controlled quenching and carbon partitioning to stabilize retained austenite.

---

## Heat Treatment Processes

• Intercritical annealing to produce ferrite and austenite phases  
• Rapid quenching to form martensite  
• Carbon partitioning to stabilize retained austenite  
• Microstructure control to optimize strength and ductility

---

## Applications

Advanced High Strength Steels are widely used in:

• Automotive crash structures  
• Structural reinforcements  
• Lightweight vehicle body panels  
• Safety-critical components

These steels help reduce vehicle weight while maintaining structural integrity.
